Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, programs, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft. Think of it as digital safety for people, companies, and systems.

Confidentiality: Ensuring that sensitive information is accessed only by authorized people.

Integrity: Making sure data is accurate and not tampered with.

Availability: Ensuring systems and data are accessible to authorized users when needed.

Authentication: Verifying the identity of users or devices before granting access.

Authorization: Controlling what an authenticated user can do or access.

Use Strong Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols; avoid obvious passwords.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security beyond passwords.

Keep Software Updated: Updates often patch security vulnerabilities.

Be Cautious with Emails/Links: Don’t click suspicious links or download unknown attachments.

Backup Your Data: Regular backups protect against ransomware or accidental loss.

Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use strong encryption (WPA3/WPA2) and a complex password.

Install Security Software: Use antivirus and anti-malware programs.

Monitor Accounts: Regularly check for unusual activity in your accounts.
